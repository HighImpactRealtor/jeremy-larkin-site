— The Diagnosis

You've been told you have to pick two. That's the lie.

The industry's playbook is simple: if you want more income, grind more hours. The wellness world says the opposite: protect your peace, don't worry about the money. Both camps are wrong. You can have all three — more income, less chaos, more time — but not on the model you're running. Here's what's actually breaking, in the words of the agents I'm sitting down with every week.

01

You're producing more and keeping less.

More closings, more overhead, more chaos — and somehow your take-home isn't moving the way it should. That's not a sales problem. That's a model problem.

02

If you stop, the income stops.

That's not a business. That's a really expensive job. And it doesn't matter how many deals you close — you don't actually own anything until that's no longer true.

03

The wins at work are funded by losses at home.

Every breakthrough quarter has a quiet receipt no one's adding up — missed dinners, short tempers, a marriage running on fumes, kids who've stopped asking when you'll be there.

04

Your health is the line item you keep deferring.

The gym, the sleep, the stress, the body — all of it on the back burner until "after this next deal." There is no "after." There's only what you're building habits to deserve.

05

"More leads" is the wrong prescription.

You don't need more leads. You're sitting on a database of people who already trust you, and you're ignoring it because you're too busy chasing strangers.

06

You already know. That's the hardest part.

Most of you reading this have known for a while. The information isn't the gap. The structure to actually act on it is.

— About

I scaled past 100 deals and got my life back. I know because I had to lose both first.

Last year I personally sold 25 homes, ran a team, got married, skied 25 days, and rode my mountain bike on random Tuesday afternoons. None of that was an accident — and none of it would've been possible the way I used to run this thing.

Twenty-one years ago I sold my first house in St. George. I've sold over 2,200 since. Scaled from 33 deals a year to 46, then broke 100. Became known as the voice of St. George real estate. Built a team. Got the awards. Did the thing.

And during the highest-producing stretch of my career, my entire personal life melted down in front of me. My marriage ended. My assistant slept at her desk one night because I couldn't stop pushing. I sat on my business partner Jesse's floor and cried — more than once.

"I'd built the most successful version of the wrong thing. It wasn't a business. It was a really expensive job."

Here's the part I don't skip: the work I'd already done as an operator — the systems, the rhythms, the architecture under the production — is what carried me through. If I hadn't built it that way, we would've gone bankrupt while I fell apart. The business held me up when I couldn't hold it up.

I rebuilt — the production AND the life. Married Jen in August 2025, both of us stronger for what we walked through. Four kids, ages 19 to 26. A three-legged lab named Max. A business that funds the life now, instead of fighting it.

That's what I help other high-producing agents build. Not because I read it in a book. Because I've been the version of you who was winning and quietly losing — and I'm not interested in pretending the first one was sustainable.

— The Path

Three moves. One business that actually works.

Everything I teach lives in three movements. Each one solves one of the three things you actually came here for. Skip any of them and the model collapses.

I.
More Income

Break the Ceiling

Find the income you're already leaving on the table — in your database, your sphere, your pricing, your conversion. Most agents don't have a lead problem. They have a leverage problem. We fix that first.

II.
Less Chaos

Build the Architecture

Replace hustle with structure. Predictable lead flow, real follow-up, rhythms you don't have to white-knuckle. The business runs on a system, not on adrenaline — so it doesn't break every time you take a weekend off.

III.
More Time

Reclaim Your Life

Get your dinners back. Your health back. Your weekends, your marriage, your hobbies. A business that funds the rest of your life instead of competing with it. This is the whole point — not a bonus round.
